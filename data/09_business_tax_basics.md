# Business Tax Basics

## Overview
Taxes are mandatory financial contributions collected by governments from businesses based on their income, transactions, or assets. Understanding business tax obligations is essential for compliance, financial planning, and avoiding penalties.

---

## Common Types of Business Taxes

### 1. Corporate Income Tax
Tax levied on a company's net profit.

> **Taxable Income = Revenue − Allowable Deductions**

- Tax rates vary by country and business structure
- Examples: US federal corporate tax rate (21%), UK corporation tax (25%)
- Filing deadlines vary — typically annually

---

### 2. Value Added Tax (VAT) / Sales Tax
A consumption tax applied at each stage of the supply chain.

- **VAT** — Common in UK, EU, and most of the world
- **Sales Tax** — Common in the US (state-level)
- Businesses collect VAT/sales tax on sales and remit it to the government
- Businesses can typically reclaim VAT paid on purchases (input tax)

> **VAT Payable = Output VAT (on sales) − Input VAT (on purchases)**

---

### 3. Payroll Taxes
Taxes related to employing staff.

- Employer's share of social security/national insurance
- Income tax withheld from employee wages (PAYE in the UK)
- Unemployment insurance contributions

---

### 4. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
Tax on profit from selling a business asset (e.g., property, investments).

- Applies when the selling price exceeds the cost basis
- Rates vary depending on asset type and holding period

---

### 5. Self-Employment Tax
For sole traders and partnerships — covers social security and Medicare/National Insurance contributions on business income.

---

## Tax Deductible Expenses (Common)
Businesses can reduce taxable income with legitimate business expenses:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Staff costs | Salaries, NI contributions, pension contributions |
| Premises | Rent, rates, utilities |
| Equipment | Computers, machinery (via depreciation/capital allowances) |
| Travel | Business mileage, flights, accommodation |
| Professional fees | Accountant, legal fees |
| Marketing | Advertising, website costs |
| Finance costs | Bank charges, loan interest |

> **Always retain receipts and records to substantiate deductions.**

---

## Tax Accounting Methods

| Method | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Cash Basis** | Record income/expenses when cash is received/paid | Small businesses |
| **Accrual Basis** | Record income/expenses when earned/incurred | Most businesses |

---

## Tax Calendar (Generic)
- **Monthly/Quarterly** — VAT returns and payroll tax filings
- **Annually** — Corporate income tax return and payment
- **As incurred** — Capital gains tax on asset disposals

---

## Key Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Tax Year** | The 12-month period for which taxes are calculated |
| **Tax Return** | Document filed with tax authority reporting income and tax due |
| **Tax Liability** | Total amount of tax owed |
| **Tax Credit** | Direct reduction in tax owed (more valuable than a deduction) |
| **Deferred Tax** | Tax obligation arising in a future period |
| **Tax Audit** | Government review of a business's tax records |

---

## Best Practices
- Keep financial records for at least 5–7 years (varies by jurisdiction)
- Separate business and personal bank accounts
- Make estimated/advance tax payments to avoid penalties
- Work with a qualified accountant or tax advisor
- File on time — late filing penalties can be significant
