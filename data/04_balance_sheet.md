# Balance Sheet

## Overview
A Balance Sheet is a financial statement that provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a **specific point in time**. It lists what the business owns (assets), what it owes (liabilities), and what belongs to the owners (equity).

> **Assets = Liabilities + Equity**

This equation must always balance — hence the name "Balance Sheet."

---

## Purpose
- Assess the financial health and stability of a business
- Show liquidity: can the business meet short-term obligations?
- Show solvency: does the business have more assets than liabilities?
- Used by investors, lenders, and management for decision-making
- Required for tax filings and regulatory compliance

---

## Structure of a Balance Sheet

### Section 1: Assets
Everything the company owns or controls with economic value.

#### Current Assets *(expected to convert to cash within 12 months)*
- Cash & Cash Equivalents
- Accounts Receivable
- Inventory
- Prepaid Expenses
- Short-term Investments

#### Non-Current Assets *(long-term)*
- Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E)
- Intangible Assets (patents, trademarks, goodwill)
- Long-term Investments
- Deferred Tax Assets

---

### Section 2: Liabilities
All financial obligations the company owes to external parties.

#### Current Liabilities *(due within 12 months)*
- Accounts Payable
- Short-term Loans / Line of Credit
- Accrued Expenses
- Deferred Revenue
- Current Portion of Long-term Debt

#### Non-Current Liabilities *(due after 12 months)*
- Long-term Loans / Bonds Payable
- Deferred Tax Liabilities
- Lease Obligations
- Pension Obligations

---

### Section 3: Equity
The residual interest in the business after liabilities are deducted from assets.

- Owner's Capital / Paid-in Capital
- Retained Earnings
- Additional Paid-in Capital
- Treasury Stock (negative)
- Other Comprehensive Income

|---|---|

| *Non-Current Assets* | |

| **LIABILITIES** | |
| Accounts Payable | $15,000 |

| **TOTAL LIABILITIES + EQUITY** | **$120,000** |

## Key Ratios from the Balance Sheet

| Ratio | Formula | Indicates |
|---|---|---|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | Short-term liquidity |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Liabilities / Total Equity | Financial leverage |
| Working Capital | Current Assets − Current Liabilities | Operational liquidity |
| Asset Turnover | Revenue / Total Assets | Efficiency of asset use |

---

## Balance Sheet vs. P&L

| | Balance Sheet | P&L Statement |
|---|---|---|
| **Time** | A single date (snapshot) | A period of time |
| **Shows** | Financial position | Profitability |
| **Key Items** | Assets, liabilities, equity | Revenue, expenses, profit |

---

## Best Practices
- Prepare at the end of every accounting period
- Ensure retained earnings tie back to the P&L net income
- Verify the balance sheet equation holds (Assets = Liabilities + Equity)
- Review asset quality — are receivables collectible? Is inventory moving?
