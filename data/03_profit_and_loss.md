# Profit & Loss Statement (P&L)

## Overview
A Profit & Loss statement — also known as an **Income Statement** — is a financial report that summarizes a company's revenues, costs, and expenses over a specific accounting period. It shows whether a business is making a profit or incurring a loss.

> **Net Profit (or Loss) = Total Revenue − Total Expenses**

---

## Purpose
- Measure business profitability over a period
- Identify areas of high cost or underperformance
- Inform strategic decisions (pricing, cost-cutting, investment)
- Required by investors, lenders, and tax authorities
- Compare performance across periods or against competitors

---

## Structure of a P&L Statement

### 1. Revenue (Income)
All income earned from core business operations.

- Sales Revenue
- Service Income
- Subscription Fees
- Other Operating Income

### 2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs associated with producing the goods or services sold.

- Raw materials
- Direct labor
- Manufacturing overhead

> **Gross Profit = Revenue − COGS**

### 3. Operating Expenses
Indirect costs of running the business.

- Salaries & Wages
- Rent & Utilities
- Marketing & Advertising
- Depreciation & Amortization
- Insurance
- Office Supplies

> **Operating Profit (EBIT) = Gross Profit − Operating Expenses**

### 4. Other Income & Expenses
Non-operational items:

- Interest income / Interest expense
- Gains or losses on asset sales
- Foreign exchange gains/losses

### 5. Tax
- Corporate income tax or applicable business tax

> **Net Profit = Operating Profit + Other Income − Tax**

---

## Sample P&L Layout

| Line Item | Amount (USD) |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $150,000 |
| Cost of Goods Sold | ($60,000) |
| **Gross Profit** | **$90,000** |
| Operating Expenses | ($50,000) |
| **Operating Profit (EBIT)** | **$40,000** |
| Interest Expense | ($2,000) |
| **Pre-Tax Profit** | **$38,000** |
| Income Tax (25%) | ($9,500) |
| **Net Profit** | **$28,500** |

---

## Key Metrics Derived from P&L

| Metric | Formula |
|---|---|
| Gross Profit Margin | (Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100 |
| Operating Profit Margin | (Operating Profit / Revenue) × 100 |
| Net Profit Margin | (Net Profit / Revenue) × 100 |

---

## Reporting Periods
- Monthly (internal management use)
- Quarterly (investor and board reporting)
- Annually (statutory and tax reporting)

---

## Best Practices
- Compare P&L across periods to identify trends
- Use budgeted vs. actual comparisons
- Ensure revenue and expenses are recorded in the correct period (accrual basis)
- Review COGS carefully — it directly drives profitability
