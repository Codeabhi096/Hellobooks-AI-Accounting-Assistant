# Chart of Accounts (COA)

## Overview
A Chart of Accounts is a structured list of all financial accounts used by a business to record transactions. It serves as the foundation of the accounting system — every journal entry, invoice, and expense maps to an account in the COA.

> Think of it as the **index** of your entire financial record system.

---

## Account Categories

Accounts are grouped into five major categories:

| # | Category | Type | Found In |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Assets** | Balance Sheet | What the business owns |
| 2 | **Liabilities** | Balance Sheet | What the business owes |
| 3 | **Equity** | Balance Sheet | Owner's stake |
| 4 | **Revenue** | P&L | Income earned |
| 5 | **Expenses** | P&L | Costs incurred |

## Numbering Conventions
- Use a **4–6 digit** numbering system for flexibility
- Group by category prefix (1xxx = Assets, 2xxx = Liabilities, etc.)
- Leave gaps between codes (e.g., 1010, 1020) to allow future additions
- Use sub-accounts for detailed tracking (e.g., 5100.01 = Executive Salaries)

---

## Designing a Good COA
- Keep it as simple as possible — avoid excessive granularity
- Align it with your financial reporting needs
- Use consistent naming conventions
- Review and update annually as the business grows
- Mirror the structure of your P&L and Balance Sheet
