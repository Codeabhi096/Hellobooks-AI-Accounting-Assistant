# Accounts Receivable & Accounts Payable

## Overview
Accounts Receivable (AR) and Accounts Payable (AP) are two sides of the same transaction — one from the seller's perspective, one from the buyer's. Together they represent the short-term cash obligations a business manages daily.

---

## Accounts Receivable (AR)

### What is AR?
Accounts Receivable represents money **owed to your business** by customers for goods or services already delivered but not yet paid for. It is recorded as a **Current Asset** on the Balance Sheet.

### AR Lifecycle
1. Goods/services delivered to customer
2. Invoice issued
3. Invoice recorded in AR ledger
4. Customer makes payment
5. Payment applied to invoice — AR balance reduced
6. Reconcile with bank statement

### Key AR Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Ideal Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) | (AR / Revenue) × Days | Lower = faster collection |
| AR Turnover | Revenue / Average AR | Higher = efficient collection |
| Bad Debt Ratio | Bad Debts / Revenue × 100 | Lower = fewer write-offs |

### Managing AR Effectively
- Set clear payment terms upfront
- Send invoices immediately after delivery
- Automate payment reminders
- Offer early payment discounts (e.g., 2/10 Net 30)
- Regularly review an aged AR report

---

## Accounts Payable (AP)

### What is AP?
Accounts Payable represents money **your business owes to suppliers** for goods or services received but not yet paid for. It is recorded as a **Current Liability** on the Balance Sheet.

### AP Lifecycle
1. Purchase order (PO) issued to supplier
2. Goods/services received
3. Supplier invoice received
4. Invoice matched to PO and delivery (3-way match)
5. Invoice approved for payment
6. Payment made by due date
7. AP ledger updated

### Key AP Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Ideal Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) | (AP / COGS) × Days | Higher = longer payment runway |
| AP Turnover | COGS / Average AP | Measures payment frequency |

### Managing AP Effectively
- Centralise all supplier invoices in one system
- Perform 3-way matching before approving payments
- Take advantage of early payment discounts where cash allows
- Negotiate extended payment terms with key suppliers
- Avoid late payments — protect supplier relationships and credit rating

---

## AR vs. AP: Side-by-Side Comparison

| | Accounts Receivable | Accounts Payable |
|---|---|---|
| **Perspective** | Money owed **to** you | Money you owe **others** |
| **Balance Sheet** | Current Asset | Current Liability |
| **Impact on Cash** | Increases cash when collected | Decreases cash when paid |
| **Key Document** | Sales Invoice | Purchase Invoice |
| **Risk** | Bad debts, late collection | Late payment penalties |

---

## Working Capital Connection
Both AR and AP directly affect **Working Capital**:

> **Working Capital = Current Assets − Current Liabilities**
> **Working Capital = (Cash + AR + Inventory) − (AP + Short-term Debt)**

- Collecting AR faster **improves** working capital
- Paying AP later (within terms) **preserves** working capital
