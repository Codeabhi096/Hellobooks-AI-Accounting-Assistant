# Invoices

## Overview
An invoice is a formal commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, requesting payment for goods or services provided. It serves as a legally binding record of a transaction and is essential for accounts receivable and accounts payable management.

---

## Types of Invoices

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Standard Invoice** | The most common type; issued after goods/services are delivered |
| **Proforma Invoice** | A preliminary invoice sent before work begins; an estimate |
| **Credit Note** | Issued to reduce the amount owed (e.g., returns, overpayments) |
| **Debit Note** | Issued to increase the amount owed |
| **Recurring Invoice** | Automatically generated at regular intervals for ongoing services |
| **Timesheet Invoice** | Used by freelancers/contractors; details hours worked |

---

## Key Components of an Invoice

A valid invoice should include:

1. **Invoice Number** — Unique identifier for tracking and reference
2. **Invoice Date** — Date the invoice was issued
3. **Due Date** — Payment deadline (e.g., Net 30, Net 60)
4. **Seller Information** — Name, address, contact details, tax/VAT number
5. **Buyer Information** — Client name, address, contact details
6. **Line Items** — Description, quantity, unit price, and subtotal per item
7. **Subtotal** — Sum before taxes and discounts
8. **Taxes/VAT** — Applied tax rates and amounts
9. **Discounts** — Any reductions applied
10. **Total Amount Due** — Final payable amount
11. **Payment Terms** — Accepted payment methods and conditions
12. **Notes/Terms** — Late payment penalties, special conditions

---

## Payment Terms Explained

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Net 30 | Full payment due within 30 days |
| Net 60 | Full payment due within 60 days |
| 2/10 Net 30 | 2% discount if paid within 10 days, otherwise full in 30 |
| Due on Receipt | Payment expected immediately |
| EOM | End of Month |

---

## Invoice Lifecycle

1. **Create** — Seller prepares the invoice after delivery
2. **Send** — Delivered via email, post, or accounting software
3. **Receive** — Buyer records it in accounts payable
4. **Approve** — Buyer verifies accuracy
5. **Pay** — Payment is made by due date
6. **Reconcile** — Both parties update their books
7. **Archive** — Stored for audit and tax purposes

---

## Overdue Invoices
- Send a payment reminder before the due date
- Issue a follow-up after the due date
- Apply late payment fees if stated in terms
- Escalate to collections if unresolved

---

## Best Practices
- Use sequential invoice numbering
- Send invoices promptly after delivery
- Follow up on unpaid invoices regularly
- Use accounting software to automate invoice creation and tracking
- Keep copies of all sent and received invoices
